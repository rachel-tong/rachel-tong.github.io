About Me 

My name is Rachel Tong, and I'm an aspiring professional engineer. I am interested in neuroengineering and neuromodulation fields of biomedical engineering. Throughout college, I worked in Neural Interfaces Research, trying to get an understanding of the neural tissue and how to develop devices that could not only record information from but also stimulate the brain. I hope to take the skills and knowledge from that experience to fuel additional learning in the field of neuroengineering. I am also interested in orthopedic biomechanics. My passion for orthopedics stems from my own experiences as I have two metal plates in my forearm from when I broke my arm in gymnastics. That surgery allowed me to heal effectively and completely and allowed me to continue as a gymnast. This is what actually got me interested in biomedical engineering in the first place and I wanted to make devices that could help other people just like someone created this device to help me heal. 

 

I thoroughly enjoy working on difficult problems and pushing the edge of technology. I like working on difficult problems and learning new skills. With that, I really like working with people - I've found it an effective way to not only gain new skills but also teach others what I have learned. In the past, I was a Teaching Assistant at the University of Wisconsin, where I mentored young engineers learn the skills to help solve future healthcare problems.
