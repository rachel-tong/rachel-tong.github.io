About Me

My name is Rachel Tong, and I'm an aspiring professional engineer in the biomedical engineering and medical device industry. One area I am interested in is neuroengineering. Throughout college, I worked in Neural Interfaces Research, researching and developing devices that can record information from the brain and also target specific neurons for stimulation. I have hands-on experience with in-vivo animal experiments testing our devices and collecting data from them. I hope to take the skills and knowledge from that experience to fuel additional learning in the field of neuroengineering.

I am also interested in orthopedic biomechanics. My passion for orthopedics stems from my own experiences as I have two metal plates in my forearm from when I broke my arm in gymnastics. That surgery allowed me to heal effectively and completely, and allowed me to continue as a gymnast. This is what generated my initial interest in biomedical engineering - it made me want to make devices that could help other people, just like someone created this device to help me.

I thoroughly enjoy working on difficult problems and pushing the edge of technology. With that, I really like working with people - I've found it an effective way to not only gain new skills but also teach others what I have learned. In the past, I was a Teaching Assistant at the University of Wisconsin, where I mentored young engineers learn the skills to help solve future healthcare problems.
